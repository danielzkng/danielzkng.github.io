Research

I am currently doing research with Professor Frank Pfenning at Carnegie Mellon University. My research involves implementing a set of parallel algorithms in a new functional language which expresses parallelism through futures. In doing so, we hope to confirm their theoretical asymptotic complexity, both in terms of the work done when run on one processor as well as the span when given an infinite number of processors.

My initial research at the University of Washington was in the field of biomedical engineering and neuroscience. I worked on optimizing code in MATLAB for reconstructing images from plane-wave ultrasound scan data to achieve a better frame rate.
